Podcasts

  • The Microsoft Research Podcast is pretty interesting, because you rarely get researchers talking about their journey.
  • Masters of Scale by Reid Hoffman does a great job at conveying a message while telling a story in a compelling and entertaining way. Plus, the guests alone are worth tuning in, with Sheryl Sandberg and Reed Hastings to name a few.
  • In Worklife Adam Grant talks about companies that make their workplace special in order to foster creativity, encourage criticism, and support a healthy work-life balance. This is not specific to the tech industry, but extremely thought-provoking, and definitely time well spent.
  • Hanselminutes by Scott Hanselman covers a very diverse set of topics related to technology.
  • Packet Pushers - Datanauts mostly focuses on infrastructure (cloud networking, storage, compute, etc.)
  • PodCtl covers the latest in the Kubernetes ecosystem.
  • Various O'Reilly podcasts such as
    • O'Reilly Data Show
    • O'Reilly Programming
    • O'Reilly Radar covers emerging technologies
  • Software Engineering Daily has been growing in popularity. It's great if you want to get an idea of a field, say web development, or caching in distributed systems, you name it.
